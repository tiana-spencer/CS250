# CS250

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
- Collecting feedback is helpful when interpretting and implementing user needs because it is a way to gain insight to the user's experience with the program. From there you will have a better understanding of how it is used and the problems that users may encounter when using it. Creating user stories can help because they provide a simplified explanation of who uses the product, what features they would like to have with it, and how those features solve problems and/or add value within the product. 

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
- Incremental development is very useful because focusing on smaller sections of the work at a time allows for more care and focus to be applied, and it is good to test your work frequently to ensure functionality and fix mistakes along the way. It is more efficient to check your work as you go and have more confidence in its security when integrating it all together later than it is to complete the program and then have to look back over the entire thing. Frequent product reviews and requirement evaluations are also helpful because they ensure that the product stays up-to-date with user and business needs.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
- A good team member is accountable: they work productively to complete tasks and fix mistakes or problems. They are committed to developing a program/product with the most quality possible and ensuring customer/business satisfaction. They are also cooperative and communicate well with their colleagues to face challenges and accomplish goals as a team.
